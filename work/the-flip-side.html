At the Young Editors’ Summit 2014, we were required to design a newsletter on the topic ‘Education & Empowerment’ in two days, including both research and design. My team, Kamya Yadav, Zoya Hassan, Sakhi Saxena, and I, went to the British Council Library, the American Centre, and leading non-government organizations like Sunaay Foundation to write articles, click pictures, and discuss ideas with reputed professionals in various fields.

The Flip Side included articles touching topics from globalization to education rights, and a community-curated people's voice section called Pop Voice.

It was designed using Adobe InDesign, with headings in FF Enzo and body in Minion Pro, delivered by TypeKit.

The human eye loves contrast.

The exclamation mark in ‘S!DE’ serves as a metaphor for ‘I’, and the word has been rotated 90 degrees, i.e. “flipped”, for a greater use of metaphors. The name of the newsletter, The Flip Side, also serves as a curiosity factor that encourages readers to flip and see what’s inside.

A wide array of tasteful colors and bold typefaces were used in the newsletter to increate visibility from distances.

The brandings of The Flip Side and The Times of India, the most read newspaper of the country, have been blurred to 20 pixels. The difference is clear.
